Life

Outside of work.

Outside research and engineering, I try to keep a life that is a little slower, a little more physical, and a little more attentive to the world around me.

Travel is probably the easiest way for me to reset my mind. I like arriving in a new city with no strong agenda, walking for a long time, noticing storefronts, public spaces, street patterns, and the small visual details that make one place feel different from another. Those moments tend to stay with me more than any checklist.

Photography fits naturally into that. I like taking photos not just to document where I was, but to hold onto a particular feeling of light, texture, or stillness. A lot of the images I enjoy most are quiet ones: corners of a city, reflections, late afternoon shadows, or scenes that feel balanced without trying too hard.

I also like having sports in my life because they bring a different kind of attention. Badminton gives me speed, rhythm, and fast decision-making; tennis feels more patient and technical, with a satisfying sense of gradual improvement. Both help me stay active and clear-headed, and both are a good counterweight to long hours spent reading, coding, and thinking.

A few things I enjoy

  • Travel that leaves room for wandering
  • Photography with quiet composition
  • Badminton for speed and rhythm
  • Tennis for patience and form
